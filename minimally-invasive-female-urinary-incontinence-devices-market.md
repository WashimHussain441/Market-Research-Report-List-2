<p><p>The minimally invasive female urinary incontinence devices market comprises two primary types: external and internal urinary incontinence devices. Internal devices, including sling systems and neuromodulation therapies, are surgically implanted to provide more permanent solutions by supporting the pelvic structure or modulating bladder function. Both types aim to enhance the quality of life for women experiencing urinary incontinence.</p></p>
