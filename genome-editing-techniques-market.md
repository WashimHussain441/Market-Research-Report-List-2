<p><p>The genome editing techniques market consists of several key types, including Zinc Finger Nucleases (ZFNs), Transcription Activator-like Effector Nucleases (TALENs), and Clustered Regularly Interspaced Short Palindromic Repeats (CRISPR). ZFNs use engineered proteins to induce DNA double-strand breaks, while TALENs employ customizable proteins for specific gene targeting. CRISPR, a more versatile and widely adopted method, utilizes RNA-guided endonucleases for precise modifications. The market also includes other innovative techniques that enhance gene editing efficiency and application across biotechnology and therapeutic fields.</p></p>
