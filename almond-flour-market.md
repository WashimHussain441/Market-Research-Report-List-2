<p><p>The almond flour market is categorized into natural almond flour, blanched almond flour, and others. Natural almond flour retains the skins of almonds, providing a coarser texture and a slightly richer flavor, often favored in health foods. Blanched almond flour is made from almonds with skins removed, offering a finer texture and lighter color, ideal for baking. The "others" category encompasses specialty flours like flavored or combined nut blends, catering to diverse consumer preferences and culinary applications.</p></p>
