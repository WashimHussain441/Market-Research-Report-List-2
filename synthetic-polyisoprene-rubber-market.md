<p><p>Synthetic polyisoprene rubber is primarily categorized into two types based on the configuration of its polymer chains: cis and trans. The cis type features the polymer chains arranged in a way that creates superior elasticity and resilience, making it ideal for tire manufacturing and flexible applications. In contrast, trans polyisoprene has a more linear structure, which enhances its heat resistance and durability, making it suitable for industrial applications. Both types serve distinct markets, catering to different performance needs in various industries.</p></p>
