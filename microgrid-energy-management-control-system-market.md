<p><p>Microgrid Energy Management Control Systems can be categorized into two main market types: centralized and decentralized control structures. Centralized control systems involve a single, central unit managing energy distribution and resource allocation, optimizing efficiency and ensuring cohesive operation across the microgrid. Conversely, decentralized control structures empower individual nodes or generators to operate autonomously, enhancing flexibility and resilience. This allows for localized decision-making and quicker adaptation to changes, making it suitable for dynamic environments with diverse energy resources.</p></p>
